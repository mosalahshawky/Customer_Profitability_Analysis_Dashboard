# Customer Profitability Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer profitability rather than just overall sales performance.
The goal is to identify which customers contribute the most to profit, which ones are loss-making, and how factors like discounts impact business performance.

Coming from a background in accounting, this project helped me move beyond working with numbers and start interpreting data from a business perspective.

---

## Tools & Technologies

* Power BI
* DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
* CSV Dataset

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains retail transaction data, including customer, product, and order-level information.

Key fields used in this analysis:

* Customer Name
* Sales
* Profit
* Discount
* Segment

This structure allows for analyzing both revenue generation and profitability at the customer level.

---

## Dashboard Features

* **Customer Profitability Analysis**

  * Comparison between sales and profit per customer
  * Identification of high-value and loss-making customers

* **Discount Impact Analysis**

  * Relationship between discount levels and profitability
  * Detection of patterns affecting margins

* **Customer Ranking**

  * Highlighting top-performing customers
  * Identifying customers generating losses

* **Segment Analysis**

  * Profit distribution across customer segments

* **Interactive Filtering**

  * Allows exploration by different dimensions

---

## Key Insights

* **Revenue does not always translate into profit**
  Some customers generate high sales but relatively low profit, showing that revenue alone is not a reliable performance indicator.

* **Presence of loss-making customers**
  Several customers generate negative profit, meaning the business is losing money on certain transactions.

* **Dependency on average customers**
  Most customers fall within a mid-range of sales and profit, indicating reliance on a broad base rather than a few key clients.

* **Negative relationship between discount and profit**
  Higher discounts are generally associated with lower profitability, suggesting a strong impact of pricing strategy.

* **High-value customers drive profitability**
  A small group of customers contributes significantly to total profit and should be prioritized.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Review and optimize discount strategies to protect profit margins
* Investigate and address loss-making customers
* Focus on retaining and growing high-profit customers
* Balance revenue growth with profitability, not just sales volume
